## The Question: Why Pray if God is Sovereign?

If God has ordained all things…  
If He knows every word before we speak…  
If His will cannot be changed…

Then why pray?

> **Matthew 6:8 (NASB1995)**  
> *“So do not be like them; for your Father knows what you need before you ask Him.”*

> **Psalm 139:4 (NASB1995)**  
> *“Even before there is a word on my tongue, Behold, O LORD, You know it all.”*

The question is not whether God knows…  
The question is why God commands what He already knows.

## God Does Not Change — Yet Responds

> **Numbers 23:19 (NASB1995)**  
> *“God is not a man, that He should lie, Nor a son of man, that He should repent; Has He said, and will He not do it? Or has He spoken, and will He not make it good?”*

> **1 Samuel 15:29 (NASB1995)**  
> *“Also the Glory of Israel will not lie or change His mind; for He is not a man that He should change His mind.”*

The doctrine of God’s immutability forces the question:
If God does not change, what place can prayer possibly have?

John Calvin addresses this directly:

> *“Believers do not pray with the view of informing God about things unknown to Him, or of exciting Him to do His duty, or of urging Him as though He were reluctant. On the contrary, they pray in order that they may arouse themselves to seek Him… that they may exercise their faith in meditating on His promises… and that they may relieve themselves from their anxieties by pouring them into His bosom.”*  
>
> [Calvin Institutes](https://ccel.org/ccel/calvin/institutes/institutes.v.xxi.html)

Calvin removes the misconception at its root:
Prayer is not given because God lacks knowledge, willingness, or clarity.

Rather, prayer is given because **we lack alignment, dependence, and understanding**.

## The Purpose of Prayer: Participation in His Will

> **Matthew 6:9–10 (NASB1995)**  
> *“Pray, then, in this way: ‘Our Father who is in heaven, Hallowed be Your name. Your kingdom come. Your will be done, On earth as it is in heaven.’”*

Stephen Armstrong explains the purpose of prayer in terms that directly answer the problem:

> *“God does not require prayer in order to accomplish His will, but He requires prayer because He has determined to accomplish His will through our participation. Prayer is the means by which God brings us into alignment with what He has already decided to do.”*  
> [VBVMI - What is the purpose of prayer](https://versebyverseministry.org/bible-answers/what-is-the-purpose-of-prayer)  
>
> Additional teaching: [VBVMI - Purpose of Prayer](https://versebyverseministry.org/lessons/purpose_in_prayer)

Armstrong’s emphasis is critical:

- God’s will is **not waiting on us**
- But God has chosen that His will will often be **carried out through us**

This moves prayer out of the realm of **influence**
and into the realm of **participation**.

## Prayer as an Ordained Means

> **Ephesians 1:11 (NASB1995)**  
> *“also we have obtained an inheritance, having been predestined according to His purpose who works all things after the counsel of His will,”*

If God works *all things* according to His will, then prayer must be included within that *“all things.”*

John Piper makes this explicit:

> *“God has ordained that He will do some things in response to prayer that He will not do otherwise. Prayer is not an afterthought in the plan of God. It is part of the plan.”*  
> [John Piper - Sovereignty of God and Prayer](https://www.desiringgod.org/articles/the-sovereignty-of-god-and-prayer)

This is not a contradiction of sovereignty—it is an expression of it.

God ordains:

- the **end** (what will happen)
- and the **means** (how it will happen)

Prayer belongs to the latter.

## A Biblical Case Study: Abraham

> **Genesis 18:17 (NASB1995)**  
> *“The LORD said, ‘Shall I hide from Abraham what I am about to do…’”*

In this moment, God reveals His plan—not because He needs counsel, but because He intends to involve Abraham.

> **Genesis 18:23 (NASB1995)**  
> *“Abraham came near and said, ‘Will You indeed sweep away the righteous with the wicked?’”*

Charles Spurgeon reflects on this dynamic:

> *“Prayer is the forerunner of mercy. Turn to sacred history, and you will find that scarcely ever did a great mercy come to this world unheralded by supplication… Prayer is the preface to blessing.”*  
> [Charles Spurgeon - Prayer the forerunner of mercy](https://www.spurgeon.org/resource-library/sermons/prayer-the-forerunner-of-mercy/#flipbook/)

Spurgeon does not suggest that prayer alters God’s decree—
but that God has decreed that **blessing will come through prayer**.

This is exactly what we see in Abraham:

- God determined judgment
- God revealed His plan
- God drew Abraham into prayer
- God accomplished His will

And in the process, Abraham learned the character of God.

## Praying in His Name

> **John 14:13 (NASB1995)**  
> *“Whatever you ask in My name, that will I do, so that the Father may be glorified in the Son.”*

> **1 John 5:14 (NASB1995)**  
> *“This is the confidence which we have before Him, that, if we ask anything according to His will, He hears us.”*

Calvin again provides needed clarity:

> *“Hence also it is evident that those who frame their prayers according to their own rashness… have no foundation for their prayers. But they who submit their wishes to God’s will… rest assured that their prayers are not in vain.”*  
> [Calvin Institutes - Chapter 20](https://ccel.org/ccel/calvin/institutes/institutes.v.xxi.html)

To pray *“in His name”* is not to attach words—
it is to submit the will.

## Conclusion: Prayer Exists for His Glory

> **Romans 11:36 (NASB1995)**  
> *“For from Him and through Him and to Him are all things. To Him be the glory forever. Amen.”*

Prayer finds its place within that framework:

- It is **from Him** — He gives the desire to pray
- It is **through Him** — He works through prayer
- It is **to Him** — He receives the glory

Stephen Armstrong summarizes this well:

> *“We exist for His glory and to do His will. Prayer is one of the primary ways God brings those two purposes together in the life of a believer.”*  
> [VBVMI - Purpose in Prayer](https://versebyverseministry.org/lessons/purpose_in_prayer)
