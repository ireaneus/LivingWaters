## Starting in Part 2 - Spiritual Heartburn

## **Chapter 7: *Christ’s Planet: Discovering Jesus in the Creation***

In Chapter 7, *Christ’s Planet*, David Murray presents the biblical truth that creation itself is fundamentally **Christ-centered**. The world is not a neutral or independent realm, but one that was **created through Christ, sustained by Christ, and ultimately exists for Christ**.

Drawing from passages such as Colossians 1:16–17, Murray shows that Christ is not merely introduced in the New Testament as Redeemer, but is actively involved from the very beginning. All things were made *through Him* and *for Him*, and even now, all things are held together by Him. This means that Christ’s authority extends beyond salvation—He is Lord over all creation.

Murray also highlights the unified work of the **Godhead** in creation, which parallels the work of salvation. The Father purposes and plans, the Son accomplishes and brings about the work, and the Holy Spirit sustains and perfects it. This same pattern is clearly seen in Ephesians 1:3–14, where the Father chooses, the Son redeems, and the Spirit seals and guarantees the inheritance of believers.

Ultimately, Murray emphasizes that redemption is not separate from creation, but rather the **restoration of what already belongs to Christ**. History is moving toward the summing up of all things in Him, as God restores His creation for His glory.

### ✍️ Personal Agreement and Reflection

I find myself in strong agreement with Murray’s central claim that this is truly **Christ’s world from beginning to end**. This chapter reinforces what is clearly taught in Ephesians 1—that salvation is not an isolated act, but part of a much larger, unified work of God.

What stands out most is the consistency of God’s work through the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Just as the Father planned creation, the Son brought it into being, and the Spirit sustained it, so also in salvation the Father chooses, the Son redeems, and the Spirit seals. This parallel strengthens the understanding that salvation is entirely God’s work, not something initiated or completed by man.

Murray’s emphasis helps clarify that redemption is not God reacting to human failure, but rather **God restoring what He has always purposed through Christ**. This aligns closely with Ephesians 1:10, where all things are being brought together in Christ. It highlights the completeness of God’s plan and removes any notion that salvation is partial or dependent on human contribution.

Additionally, this chapter challenges how I view the world around me. If creation itself belongs to Christ, then life is not about finding meaning independently, but about being restored to the purpose for which we were created—to live *to the praise of His glory*.

In light of this, I affirm that:

* Christ is central not only to salvation, but to all of creation
* The work of salvation reflects the same divine order seen in creation
* From beginning to end, both creation and redemption display the glory of God

This chapter ultimately deepens my understanding that everything—creation, redemption, and final restoration—is unified under one reality:

**Christ is at the center of it all.**
