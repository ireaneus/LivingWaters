## **Chapter 7 Summary: Christ’s Planet — Discovering Jesus in the Creation**

*Jesus on Every Page, David P. Murray*

Chapter 7 argues that creation itself is not merely a backdrop to redemption but an intentional revelation of Christ. Murray emphasizes that the natural world is structured, sustained, and interpreted through Jesus Christ. Creation is not neutral—it is Christ-centered in its origin, purpose, and ongoing existence.

Murray explains that Christ is present in creation in at least three ways:

1. **Christ as Creator** – All things were made through Him. Creation is not independent of Christ but originates in Him.
2. **Christ as Sustainer** – The universe continues to exist and function because Christ actively holds it together.
3. **Christ as Revealer** – Creation reflects truths about Christ’s nature, wisdom, power, and glory.

Rather than treating creation as a general revelation that vaguely points to God, Murray presses further—creation specifically points to Christ. The beauty, order, and design of the world are not random; they are expressions of the Son’s divine wisdom and authority.

He also highlights that creation prepares the categories needed to understand redemption. Light and darkness, life and death, order and chaos—all these created realities become the language through which salvation is revealed. Without creation, the gospel would lack its foundational vocabulary.

> *“Creation is the stage, the script, and the set of symbols that make redemption intelligible.”* (paraphrased from Murray)

Ultimately, Murray calls readers to read creation Christologically—to see the world not as an end in itself but as a signpost pointing to the glory of Christ.

### **Christ in Creation — An Eternal Plan Revealed**

Before creation ever began, redemption was not a reaction—it was already purposed in eternity through Christ.

> **Ephesians 1:4–5 (NASB1995)**
> *“just as He chose us in Him before the foundation of the world, that we would be holy and blameless before Him. In love He predestined us to adoption as sons through Jesus Christ to Himself, according to the kind intention of His will,”*

> **1 Peter 1:20 (NASB1995)**
> *“For He was foreknown before the foundation of the world, but has appeared in these last times for the sake of you”*

**Creation is not separate from Christ—it is the first visible expression of a plan centered on Him.**

---

### **Christ as the Agent of Creation**

> **John 1:1–3 (NASB1995)**
> *“In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. He was in the beginning with God. All things came into being through Him, and apart from Him nothing came into being that has come into being.”*

> **Colossians 1:16–17 (NASB1995)**
> *“For by Him all things were created, both in the heavens and on earth, visible and invisible… all things have been created through Him and for Him. He is before all things, and in Him all things hold together.”*

Christ is not merely present in creation—**creation exists through Him and for Him**.

---

### **Christ in the Light of Creation**

> **Genesis 1:3–4 (NASB1995)**
> *“Then God said, ‘Let there be light’; and there was light. God saw that the light was good; and God separated the light from the darkness.”*

> **John 8:12 (NASB1995)**
> *“Then Jesus again spoke to them, saying, ‘I am the Light of the world; he who follows Me will not walk in the darkness, but will have the Light of life.’”*

The very first act of creation—light breaking into darkness—establishes a category that is ultimately fulfilled in Christ.

---

### **Christ in Provision and Sustaining Creation**

> **Psalm 104:27–30 (NASB1995)**
> *“They all wait for You to give them their food in due season… You send forth Your Spirit, they are created; And You renew the face of the ground.”*

Creation depends continuously on God’s provision—fulfilled ultimately in Christ as the sustainer of all things.

---

### **Why Six Days of Creation? — A Pattern Pointing to Christ**

> **Genesis 2:2–3 (NASB1995)**
> *“By the seventh day God completed His work which He had done, and He rested on the seventh day from all His work which He had done. Then God blessed the seventh day and sanctified it…”*

God did not need six days—He chose six days.

This establishes a divine pattern:

- Six days of work
- One day of rest

But this pattern is not merely about time—it is **about completion and rest**.

---

### **Christ as the Fulfillment of the Sabbath**

> **Hebrews 4:3 (NASB1995)**
> *“For we who have believed enter that rest, just as He has said… although His works were finished from the foundation of the world.”*

> **Hebrews 4:9–10 (NASB1995)**
> *“So there remains a Sabbath rest for the people of God. For the one who has entered His rest has himself also rested from his works, as God did from His.”*

> **Matthew 11:28 (NASB1995)**
> *“Come to Me, all who are weary and heavy-laden, and I will give you rest.”*

The seventh day is not just a conclusion—it is a **signpost**.

God’s rest in creation points forward to a greater rest:

**Rest in Christ.**

---

### **The Ongoing Seventh Day — A Deeper Insight**

In Genesis, every day ends with:

> *“And there was evening and there was morning…”*

But the seventh day does not end that way.

> **Genesis 2:2–3 (NASB1995)**
> *(No closing formula of evening and morning is given)*

This is not accidental.

It suggests that:

- God’s rest is not confined to a 24-hour period
- The seventh day continues as an **open invitation**

This aligns directly with Hebrews:

> **Hebrews 4:6–7 (NASB1995)**
> *“Therefore, since it remains for some to enter it… He again fixes a certain day, ‘Today’…”*

The rest of God is still available—**because it is fulfilled in Christ, not in a day.**
