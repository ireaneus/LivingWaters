## Genesis Chapter 1 vs. 1-2 "formless and void"

**Gen. 1:1-2**

> In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.  
> The earth was [became] formless and void, and darkness was over the surface of the deep, and the Spirit of God was moving over the surface of the waters.
> Then God said, *“Let there be light”*; and there was light.  
> God saw that the light was good; and God separated the light from the darkness.  
> God called the light day, and the darkness He called night. And there was evening and there was morning, one day.

## Devotional Segment

### “Formless and Void” — Chaos or Preparation?

One phrase in Genesis 1 has taken on a life of its own in modern thinking:

> *“The earth was formless and void…”* (Genesis 1:2)

To many ears today, that phrase sounds like chaos—disorder, meaninglessness, even something broken. But Scripture never presents God as the author of chaos. From beginning to end, He is revealed as a God of **order, purpose, and intention**.

Genesis 1:1 declares a completed truth:

> *“In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.”*

What follows is not a contradiction of that act, but an explanation of **how God ordered what He had created**.

The Hebrew phrase often translated *“formless and void”* (*tohu va-bohu*)

* *Tohu* — without form, unshaped, lacking structure
* *Bohu* — empty, unfilled, a vacuum

Together, they describe **an unformed and uninhabited state**, not moral or physical chaos.

God does not create chaos and then fix it.
He creates **space**, then fills it with meaning.

The opening days of creation show a deliberate pattern. First, God forms realms—light and darkness, sky and sea, land. Then He fills those realms—sun, moon, stars; birds and fish; animals and humanity. Order precedes fullness. Purpose precedes population.

Even light itself is introduced before the sun, reminding us that creation is not driven first by material objects, but by God’s word, power, and presence. The universe begins not with randomness, but with **intentional design**—a space prepared to be filled with life, meaning, and ultimately, people made in His image.

Genesis does not open with chaos.
It opens with **a stage being set**.

## Proofs & Notes (For Study)

### 1. God Is Not the Author of Chaos

* **1 Corinthians 14:33**

  > *“For God is not a God of confusion but of peace.”*

* **Isaiah 45:18**

  > *“He did not create it a waste place, but formed it to be inhabited.”*
  > (Same Hebrew root as *tohu*—explicitly denying chaos as God’s intent.)

These passages frame *tohu* as **incompleteness**, not disorder.

---

### 2. Meaning of *Tohu va-Bohu*

* *Tohu* — without form, unshaped, lacking structure
* *Bohu* — empty, unfilled, a vacuum

Together, they describe **an unformed and uninhabited state**, not moral or physical chaos.

The same phrase appears elsewhere (e.g., Jeremiah 4:23) where context determines meaning—but Genesis 1 provides its own interpretation through the structured days that follow.

---

### 3. Genesis 1 Shows a Clear Form–Fill Pattern

**Days 1–3: God Forms Space**

* Day 1: Light / Darkness
* Day 2: Sky / Waters
* Day 3: Land / Seas

**Days 4–6: God Fills Space**

* Day 4: Sun, Moon, Stars (to govern light and time)
* Day 5: Birds and Fish (to fill sky and sea)
* Day 6: Animals and Humanity (to fill the land)

This symmetry shows intentional design, not repair.

---

### 4. Creation Begins with Energy and Order, Not Matter Alone

* **Genesis 1:2** — *“The Spirit of God was hovering…”*
  The verb implies active presence, not absence.

* **Psalm 33:6**

  > *“By the word of the LORD the heavens were made.”*

* **Hebrews 11:3**

  > *“What is seen was not made out of things which are visible.”*

Light precedes luminaries. Word precedes matter. Order precedes form.
Creation begins with **God’s command**, not physical mechanisms.

---

### 5. Space, Time, and Purpose Are Established Together

* **Genesis 1:1** introduces the totality: *heavens and earth*
* **Genesis 1:2–3** explains function and sequence
* **Genesis 1:14** explicitly ties light to *time* (days, seasons, years)

Creation is not accidental space—it is **prepared space**, designed to be inhabited and ruled under God’s authority.
