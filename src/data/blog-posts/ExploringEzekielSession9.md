## The New Covenant Promised to Israel

[↑ Table of Contents](#table-of-contents)

> **Jeremiah 31:31–34 (NASB1995)**  
> *“Behold, days are coming,” declares the Lord, “when I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel and with the house of Judah, not like the covenant which I made with their fathers in the day I took them by the hand to bring them out of the land of Egypt, My covenant which they broke, although I was a husband to them,” declares the Lord. “But this is the covenant which I will make with the house of Israel after those days,” declares the Lord, “I will put My law within them and on their heart I will write it; and I will be their God, and they shall be My people. They will not teach again, each man his neighbor and each man his brother, saying, ‘Know the Lord,’ for they will all know Me, from the least of them to the greatest of them,” declares the Lord, “for I will forgive their iniquity, and their sin I will remember no more.”*

The New Covenant is **explicitly made with Israel and Judah**, promising:

- Internal transformation  
- Full forgiveness  
- A restored relationship with God

**Question:** *So if the New Covenant is for Israel, then why is it talked about in our communion?*

## The New Covenant Inaugurated by Christ

[↑ Table of Contents](#table-of-contents)

> **Luke 22:20 (NASB1995)**  
> *“And in the same way He took the cup after they had eaten, saying, ‘This cup which is poured out for you is the new covenant in My blood.’”*

> **Hebrews 9:15 (NASB1995)**  
> *“For this reason He is the mediator of a new covenant, so that, since a death has taken place for the redemption of the transgressions that were committed under the first covenant, those who have been called may receive the promise of the eternal inheritance.”*

Jesus’ death **established (inaugurated)** the New Covenant.

It is:

- Already **ratified by His blood**  
- Not yet **fully fulfilled nationally in Israel**

## Participants vs. Recipients

[↑ Table of Contents](#table-of-contents)

> **Romans 11:17–18 (NASB1995)**  
> *“But if some of the branches were broken off, and you, being a wild olive, were grafted in among them and became partaker with them of the rich root of the olive tree, do not be arrogant toward the branches; but if you are arrogant, remember that it is not you who supports the root, but the root supports you.”*

A key distinction:

- **Recipients** → Israel (the covenant is made with them)  
- **Participants** → The Church (sharing in its blessings)  

Believers are **grafted in**, not replacing Israel, but sharing in what flows from God’s promises.

## Spiritual Blessings Shared Now

[↑ Table of Contents](#table-of-contents)

The Church presently experiences the **spiritual blessings** of the New Covenant:

### Forgiveness of sins

> **Jeremiah 31:34 (NASB1995)**  
> *“…for I will forgive their iniquity, and their sin I will remember no more.”*

### New heart and indwelling Spirit

> **Ezekiel 36:26–27 (NASB1995)**  
> *“Moreover, I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit within you; and I will remove the heart of stone from your flesh and give you a heart of flesh. I will put My Spirit within you and cause you to walk in My statutes, and you will be careful to observe My ordinances.”*

### Direct relationship with God

> **Hebrews 8:10–11 (NASB1995)**  
> *“For this is the covenant that I will make with the house of Israel after those days, says the Lord: I will put My laws into their minds, and I will write them on their hearts. And I will be their God, and they shall be My people. And they shall not teach everyone his fellow citizen, and everyone his brother, saying, ‘Know the Lord,’ for all will know Me, from the least to the greatest of them.”*

These are realities for believers **right now in Christ**.

## The Role of the Abrahamic Promise

[↑ Table of Contents](#table-of-contents)

> **Galatians 3:13–14 (NASB1995)**  
> *“Christ redeemed us from the curse of the Law, having become a curse for us—for it is written, ‘Cursed is everyone who hangs on a tree’— in order that in Christ Jesus the blessing of Abraham might come to the Gentiles, so that we would receive the promise of the Spirit through faith.”*

The Abrahamic Covenant provides:

- The **foundation of blessing**  
- The promise that **all nations would be blessed**  

The New Covenant provides:

- The **means by which those blessings are applied**  
- Forgiveness, new life, and the Spirit

## Provoking Israel to Jealousy

[↑ Table of Contents](#table-of-contents)

> **Romans 11:11 (NASB1995)**  
> *“I say then, they did not stumble so as to fall, did they? May it never be! But by their transgression salvation has come to the Gentiles, to make them jealous.”*

> **Romans 11:13–14 (NASB1995)**  
> *“But I am speaking to you who are Gentiles. Inasmuch then as I am an apostle of Gentiles, I magnify my ministry, if somehow I might move to jealousy my fellow countrymen and save some of them.”*

God’s redemptive plan includes a present purpose in the Church:

- As Gentiles receive salvation  
- As believers experience forgiveness, the Spirit, and new life  

Israel sees what was promised to them **being experienced by others**.

This produces a **holy jealousy**, pointing back to God’s covenant faithfulness and forward to their future restoration.

## Future Fulfillment for Israel

[↑ Table of Contents](#table-of-contents)

> **Romans 11:25–27 (NASB1995)**  
> *“For I do not want you, brethren, to be uninformed of this mystery—so that you will not be wise in your own estimation—that a partial hardening has happened to Israel until the fullness of the Gentiles has come in; and so all Israel will be saved; just as it is written, ‘The Deliverer will come from Zion, He will remove ungodliness from Jacob.’ ‘This is My covenant with them, when I take away their sins.’”*

The New Covenant will be **fully and literally fulfilled** when:

- Israel is restored nationally  
- Their sins are removed  
- The promises of Jeremiah are completed in full  

God’s covenant faithfulness guarantees this outcome.

## Communion and the New Covenant

[↑ Table of Contents](#table-of-contents)

> **1 Corinthians 11:25 (NASB1995)**  
> *“In the same way He took the cup also after supper, saying, ‘This cup is the new covenant in My blood; do this, as often as you drink it, in remembrance of Me.’”*

Communion looks in three directions:

- **Back** → remembering Christ’s sacrifice  
- **Present** → participating in covenant blessings  
- **Forward** → anticipating future fulfillment  

---

## Summary Statement

[↑ Table of Contents](#table-of-contents)

The Church does not replace Israel in the New Covenant.  

Through union with Christ, believers **share in the spiritual blessings of that covenant now**, while its **full and literal fulfillment remains promised to Israel in the future**, all grounded in God’s unchanging covenant faithfulness.
